Redefining Free

My View on the New FTC Law

11-23-2019
TheCrafters001
Rant

So… The time has finally come that YouTube could die. All because they decided to break the law, and open their mouth. This would have never of happened if they said nothing. All YouTube had to do was just sit and stay quite. Nope. They thought it was a good idea to say that they were purposefully showing targeted ads to kids. Who would of thought? And this “Age Rating System” they came up with, is ALSO ignoring the law. On Purpose. Because they say they will not make enough money if they include what is said in the law.

The law states that there IS a middle ground. There is a General Audience Exception. In. The. LAW. But YouTube ignored it. And for content creators who think that moving to a new platform, like Vlare or Vimeo, think again. Whatever the law says, and someone makes something similar to the thing, the law follows. COPPA is not just confined to YouTube, it is confined to EVERYONE. There is no stopping the law.
Now, I should not be affected too much, as I target people over 13, and the fact that I don’t use cookies to collect information on people, and I don’t track people on my apps. But if the law says no, they say no. I have never had ADS on my website, never will. Hence my slogan, “Redefining Free.” But if the government has people that LITERALLY DON’T HAVE CELLPHONES, they you guys are in trouble, no matter what.

My advice? Stop Targeted Ads; Stop Tracking Cookies.
